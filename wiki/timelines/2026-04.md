# April 2026

## Week 1 (1st–7th)

April opened with the most ironic headline of 2026: Anthropic accidentally leaked Claude Code's 512,000-line TypeScript source via npm .map misconfiguration. Within hours, claw-code — a Python clean-room rewrite — hit 50K stars in 2 hours and 100K total, creating a DMCA-proof derived work.

OpenAI closed its historic $122B funding round at $852B valuation (Amazon $50B, NVIDIA/SoftBank $30B each). The company generates $2B/month revenue but still loses billions annually. Sora shutdown confirmed ($1M/day burn, Disney deal terminated). [[sources/openai]] — [[entities/claw-code]]

SpaceX acquired xAI for $250B, creating a $1.25T combined entity. [[sources/xai]]

Anthropic's Claude Mythos Preview discovered thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities including a 27-year-old OpenBSD bug. Access was restricted to Project Glasswing — 40+ partners including Microsoft, Apple, Amazon, Google, NVIDIA, CrowdStrike. $100M credits committed to fixing vulnerabilities. [[entities/claude-mythos]]

Google released Gemma 4 under Apache 2.0 — four variants (2B-31B), 31B ranked #3 on Arena AI. [[entities/gemma-4]] — [[sources/google]]

Q1 2026 VC funding hit $267B record — 80% to AI. [[topics/ai_funding]]

## Week 2 (8th–14th)

Claude Mythos triggered emergency government response. VP JD Vance and Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent held calls with CEOs of Google, Microsoft, OpenAI, xAI, CrowdStrike. UK regulators accelerated Mythos risk assessments. Powell and Bessent convened Wall Street bank CEOs over cyber risk. [[topics/ai_safety]]

Anthropic's $30B ARR confirmed — up from $9B at end-2025. 1,000+ enterprise customers at $1M+. Claude Opus 4.6 achieved 80.8% on SWE-bench, near-tie with Gemini 3.1 Pro at 80.6%. [[sources/anthropic]] — [[entities/claude-opus-4-6]]

OpenAI's Codex reached 3M weekly active users (70% month-over-month growth). GPT-5.4-Cyber released — defensive cybersecurity variant. [[entities/gpt-5.4-cyber]]

Meta released Muse Spark as its first proprietary model — breaking with open-source tradition, ranking 4th on benchmarks. [[entities/muse-spark]]

Stanford HAI 2026 report: US-China AI performance gap effectively closed. Top models within 2.7 points on Arena benchmark. [[topics/ai_companies]]

Alibaba Qwen3.6-Plus debuted at top of global usage charts. [[sources/deepseek]]

HumanX 2026 ("Davos of AI") gathered 6,500+ leaders in San Francisco. Physical AI demo by Samsanga with autonomous trucks. [[topics/agentic_ai]]

OpenAI, Anthropic, Google formed Frontier Model Forum coalition against Chinese model distillation. [[ideas/us-china-ai-fragmentation]]

**April 15-16: Stanford HAI report dominates**: Stanford's 2026 AI Index reveals US-China performance gap has collapsed to 2.7% (down from 17.5-31.6 points in May 2023), despite America spending 23x more on private AI investment ($285.9B vs $12.4B). [[topics/llm_models]] — [[topics/ai_companies]]

Anthropic releases Claude Opus 4.7 with 87.6% SWE-bench (up from 80.8%), tripled vision resolution to 3.75 megapixels, and new cyber safeguards developed from Claude Mythos safety work. [[entities/claude-opus-4-7]]

OpenAI shifts focus to "high-value professional work" with Spud model codenamed. Business revenue now 40% of sales (up from 20% two years ago). [[sources/openai]]

ASML and TSMC forecasts signal AI spending boom remains intact. ASML raises 2026 revenue outlook to €36-40B. [[topics/ai_funding]]

AI coding tools (Cursor, Claude Code, Codex) converge into unified stacks. Microsoft Agent Framework 1.0 ships with full MCP support. 84% of developers use AI coding tools daily. [[topics/github_trends]]

Google's A2A Protocol hits one-year anniversary: 150+ organizations, 22,000 GitHub stars, deployed in Azure AI Foundry and Amazon Bedrock AgentCore. [[entities/a2a-protocol]]

**April 17: Major product launches**: Anthropic launches Claude Design, directly targeting Figma/Canva design workflow. Mike Krieger (Anthropic CPO) resigns from Figma's board days before announcement. [[entities/claude-design]]

OpenAI launches GPT-Rosalind for life sciences — biology, drug discovery, translational medicine. Early access partners: Amgen, Moderna, Allen Institute, Thermo Fisher Scientific. Novo Nordisk strategic partnership. [[entities/gpt-rosalind]]

Perplexity launches "Computer" — always-on AI agent running locally on Mac mini, $200/month for Max subscribers. Manages files, controls native apps, browses web autonomously. [[entities/perplexity-computer]]

Perplexity reports 5x revenue growth to $500M, staff up only 34%. [[sources/perplexity]]

Recursive Superintelligence raises $500M seed four months after founding. [[topics/ai_funding]]

OpenAI's chief economist publishes study: 18% of US jobs face "relatively higher risk" from AI, but reorganization and expansion more likely than outright job losses. [[topics/ai_companies]]

Cerebras files for US IPO. AI chip rival to Nvidia reveals public listing plans. [[topics/ai_funding]]

**April 18: Strategic partnerships and quantum AI**: Anthropic expands partnership with Google and Broadcom for multi-gigawatt next-generation compute infrastructure. [[sources/anthropic]]

NVIDIA launches Ising — world's first open quantum AI model family. Delivers 2.5x faster and 3x more accurate quantum error correction decoding. Automates quantum processor calibration from days to hours. [[entities/ising]]

Mozilla launches Thunderbolt — enterprise self-hosted AI client. Cross-platform, any OpenAI-compatible API endpoint, native apps for web/macOS/Windows/Linux/iOS/Android. [[entities/thunderbolt]]

Stanford HAI 2026 report: China's top AI model trails US leader by only 39 Arena points (down from 300+ gap in May 2023). China leads in AI publication citations (20.6% vs 12.6%) and industrial robot installations. AI scholar inflow to US dropped 89% since 2017. [[topics/ai_companies]]
