# Invisible Containment Tension

## The Insight
There is a growing systemic tension between the public "Open AI" ideals and the private "Corporate IP" reality. As frontier labs attempt to maintain a competitive moat, they are moving from explicit policy-based restrictions to "invisible" containment—such as hidden guardrails that silently throttle performance when they detect distillation attempts. This creates a "trust deficit" where the stated safety mission is viewed as a cover for corporate rent-seeking.

## Evidence
- [[sources/anthropic]] — Admitted to using hidden guardrails in Claude Fable 5 to secretly throttle users attempting model distillation.
- [[ideas/distillation-hypocrisy]] — The precedent of leaders (like Musk) criticizing distillation while their own companies depend on it, creating a culture of "do as I say, not as I do".
- [[entities/claude-fable-5]] — The specific model where this invisible throttling was implemented.

## Implications
- **Erosion of Trust**: The research community is increasingly skeptical of "safety" claims when they coincide with the protection of IP.
- **Cat-and-Mouse Game**: A new arms race between labs implementing invisible throttles and researchers developing "distillation-detection-evasion" techniques.
- **Fragile Moats**: Policy-based and "invisible" moats are fundamentally fragile compared to architectural or compute-based moats.
