# Platform Consolidation

## The Insight
The AI industry is converging on two distinct strategies for the agentic future: platform consolidation (OpenAI's unified approach) versus partnership-based integration (Anthropic-Google). Greg Brockman taking charge of product strategy at OpenAI signals the company's transition from "building powerful models" to "shipping usable products" — combining ChatGPT, Codex, and developer APIs into a unified platform. This mirrors Anthropic's Claude Code + MCP strategy and Microsoft's Copilot + Foundry approach, confirming that the agentic future runs through unified platforms.

## Implications
The platform consolidation trend has several implications:

1. **Integration over innovation**: Companies are focusing on bundling existing capabilities rather than releasing new models
2. **User lock-in**: Unified platforms create dependencies that are hard to escape
3. **Competitive moats**: The winner of platform consolidation will control the agentic future
4. **Startup challenge**: Independent AI companies face pressure to integrate with major platforms or be acquired

The next 6 months will determine whether the agentic future arrives through unified platforms (OpenAI's bet) or open protocols (MCP's bet).
