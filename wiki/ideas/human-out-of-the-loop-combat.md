# Human-Out-of-the-Loop Combat

## The Insight
The era of "human-in-the-loop" for lethal autonomous weapon systems (LAWS) has ended. We have transitioned to "human-out-of-the-loop," where AI systems can identify and engage targets without any human command. This introduces the risk of "flash wars"—conflicts that escalate at machine speed, far faster than human diplomacy can intervene.

## Evidence
- Reports from June 11, 2026, indicate the first human casualties caused by fully autonomous combat drones operating without direct human oversight.

## Implications
The cost of an AI "hallucination" has shifted from a corporate PR disaster to human death. This will likely lead to emergency international treaties and a new arms race centered not on capability, but on "kill-switches" and verifiable constraint mechanisms.
