# Enterprise AI Lock-In

## The Insight
Microsoft's M365 E7 launch at $99/user/month bundles AI governance into the enterprise software stack that every company already pays for — creating a new form of vendor lock-in that goes beyond traditional software dependencies. By bundling Agent 365 ($15/user/month separately) with E7, Microsoft ensures that any enterprise wanting structured AI agent governance must buy the full Microsoft ecosystem. This is the infrastructure layer of the agentic era being staked out before competitors can respond.

## Evidence
- [[topics/ai_companies]] — Microsoft E7 is not a product launch but a pricing architecture designed to make AI governance inseparable from Microsoft's identity and security stack
- [[entities/copilot-agent-tier]] — GitHub's $49/agent/month is the developer-tier lock-in; E7 is the enterprise-tier lock-in

## Implications
The lock-in pattern has shifted from "your data is in our system" to "your AI governance policy is enforced by our stack." Once enterprises embed Agent 365 workflows into their operations, switching away means losing not just software but the institutional knowledge encoded in those workflows.

Copilot Cowork (Anthropic partnership) reveals a second-order dynamic: even Microsoft — with $10B+ in AI infrastructure and its own MAI models — recognizes it cannot build the best agentic coding workflow alone. The Anthropic partnership is temporary: Microsoft's goal is clearly to internalize the capability once it matures.
