# Application-Layer Shift

## The Insight
The AI competition has fundamentally shifted from model racing (who has the best benchmark scores) to application-layer differentiation (who captures workflows and vertical domains). By mid-April 2026, this shift is unmistakable: Anthropic's Claude Design directly challenges Figma's design workflow, OpenAI's GPT-Rosalind targets life sciences with Amgen/Moderna/Novo Nordisk partnerships, Perplexity's "Computer" runs locally on Mac mini as an always-on agent. The model race is giving way to workflow capture.

## Implications
This shift has profound implications for AI company strategy and valuation. Model capabilities matter less than distribution and workflow integration. Companies that can embed AI into professional workflows (design, coding, life sciences, finance) will capture more value than those competing on benchmark leadership.

For developers, the signal is clear: the era of building on a single model API is ending; the future is specialized models with genuine domain expertise integrated into specific workflows.
