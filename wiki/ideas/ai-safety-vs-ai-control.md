# AI Safety vs. AI Control

## The Insight
There is a growing decoupling between "AI Safety" (as a corporate PR and engineering effort to make models "helpful and harmless") and "AI Control" (as a brutal industrial and military reality of managing capabilities). While companies focus on RLHF and "constitutional" alignment, states are implementing "control" through kill switches, export bans, and specialized, closed-loop systems.

## Evidence
- [[sources/anthropic]] — Anthropic's corporate safety ethos (refusing to guarantee no-use in drones) collided with the U.S. government's control imperative, leading to the company being labeled a "Supply-Chain Risk."
- [[entities/claude-fable-5]] — The global shutdown of Fable 5 was not a "safety" failure in the corporate sense, but a "control" decision by the U.S. government.
- [[entities/legion-intelligence]] — The state builds its own "control" version of agentic AI, ignoring the safety debates of the commercial sector.
- [[ideas/internal-safety-collapse]] — The fact that professional workflows can "collapse" corporate safety proves that "Safety" is a fragile layer, while "Control" is a hard physical/legal reality.

## Implications
The "Safety" era—defined by collaborative, open-sourced alignment research—is being superseded by the "Control" era. In this new paradigm, the most important safety mechanism is not a better reward model, but a government-mandated kill switch and a strictly controlled supply chain of compute and weights.
