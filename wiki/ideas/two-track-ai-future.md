# Two-Track AI Future

## The Insight
The Pentagon's May 2026 exclusion of Anthropic from classified AI contracts — while awarding them to OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, AWS, Nvidia, SpaceX, Oracle, and Reflection AI — crystallizes a fundamental bifurcation in the AI industry. One track builds on defense partnerships and developer tooling dominance; the other is built on safety-first positioning and researcher-led innovation. These are not just different strategies — they represent incompatible visions of what AI is for, and the split is now structurally permanent.

## Evidence
- [[sources/anthropic]] — Anthropic was permanently excluded from IL6/IL7 classified networks after refusing to accept "any lawful use" language and insisting on guardrails against autonomous weapons and mass surveillance. Defense Secretary Hegseth labeled the company a "supply chain risk" — a designation normally reserved for foreign adversaries. The company lost access to federal contracts worth $4B+ annually.
- Pentagon dropping Claude — On May 28, 2026, the DoD began testing OpenAI and Google models to replace Claude after designating Anthropic a supply-chain risk. Claude was deeply integrated into the Maven Smart System for classified operations — replacement is not a flip-of-a-switch decision. The signal is clear: AI companies making ethical choices about military use will face enterprise opportunity costs.

- [[sources/openai]] — OpenAI secured Pentagon contracts alongside Google, Microsoft, AWS, Nvidia, SpaceX, Oracle, and Reflection AI for "lawful operational use" covering 1M+ defense personnel. This followed OpenAI's reversal on model restrictions — after mocking Anthropic's Mythos restrictions, OpenAI quietly restricted GPT-5.5 Cyber to verified "critical cyber defenders."

- [[ideas/safety-restricted-releases]] — Claude Mythos established the precedent that some models may be too capable for public release. Anthropic's exclusion from the Pentagon is the commercial consequence of that principled stance — the safety-first positioning has a $4B+ annual price tag.

- [[entities/stainless-sdk]] — Anthropic's $300M acquisition of Stainless SDK represents the platform pivot: shifting from model-only to controlling the developer tooling layer. This is the defensive move of a company locked out of defense markets — doubling down on the developer ecosystem as an alternative growth vector.


- [[entities/gemini-3-5-flash]] — Google's aggressive Flash-first strategy (76.2% on Terminal-Bench 2.1 at half the price) shows the defense track is also about developer adoption — whoever controls the developer ecosystem controls the future of AI deployment.

## Implications
The two-track future has profound implications:

1. **Talent Migration**: Top researchers who prioritize safety will continue to join Anthropic (see: Andrej Karpathy joining as Pretraining Advisor, Jan Leike joining from OpenAI safety team). Researchers who prioritize deployment scale will join OpenAI/Google.

2. **Regulatory Capture**: The Pentagon coalition creates a de facto regulatory gatekeeper. Labs outside the coalition (Anthropic, Meta, Mistral) face structural disadvantages in federal markets worth $4B+ annually.

3. **Investment Bifurcation**: Defense-adjacent AI companies will receive classified compute and data that safety-first companies cannot access. This creates a capability gap that may widen over time.

4. **Narrative Divergence**: The defense track will emphasize "operational readiness" and "real-world deployment." The safety track will emphasize "trust" and "auditable AI." These narratives will appeal to fundamentally different buyer segments.

5. **Geographic Split**: US allies aligned with Pentagon will get access to the defense track. EU regulators (denied Mythos access) will gravitate toward safety-first labs. This creates a global AI fragmentation not along East/West lines but along defense/safety lines.
