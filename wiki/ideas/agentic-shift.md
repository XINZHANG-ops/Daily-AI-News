# The Agentic Shift

## The Insight
May 21, 2026 crystallizes a fundamental transition in AI: from "tool that answers questions" to "agent that does things on your behalf." This isn't incremental — it's a categorical shift in what AI means. Google embedding agents directly into search, Meta building purchase-flow agents into Instagram, OpenAI's mathematical proof capability, Singapore positioning as the offshore AI hub — all point to the same transformation.

## Evidence
- [[sources/google]] — Google's search AI overhaul embeds conversational AI queries, "information agents" that track listings and make phone calls, and custom software interface generation — moving beyond "10 blue links" to "do it for you"
- [[entities/hatch]] — Meta's Hatch AI assistant for Instagram enables agentic shopping where users tap once and Hatch handles the entire purchase flow
- [[sources/openai]] — OpenAI's model solving the planar unit distance conjecture demonstrates AI not just answering questions but autonomously solving complex problems
- [[sources/nvidia]] — Nvidia partnering with Singapore on embodied AI research hub signals hardware moving from "compute for models" to "compute for agents in the physical world"
- [[sources/singapore]] — Singapore positioning as the offshore AI hub with OpenAI, Google, and Nvidia partnerships is about agent deployment infrastructure, not just model hosting

## Implications
The agentic shift changes the unit of value from "per query" to "per task completed." This has profound implications:
- Revenue models shift from API usage to outcomes delivered
- Competition moves from "who answers better" to "who completes more tasks"
- The skills pattern (mattpocock/skills +1,618 stars this week) shows developers building agent capabilities as reusable composable units
- The next 6 months will determine whether big platforms (Google, Meta, Apple) capture agent commerce or if an open ecosystem wins
