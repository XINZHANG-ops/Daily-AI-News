# Platform to Publisher Liability Shift

## The Insight
There is a fundamental legal shift occurring where AI providers are moving from being "platforms" (which host third-party content and enjoy immunity) to "publishers" (which create content and are liable for its accuracy). This means the "search engine" defense—claiming they are merely indexing the web—is failing when the AI generates a synthetic summary (AI Overview) that contains a falsehood.

## Evidence
- [[sources/google]] — A Munich court ruled in June 2026 that Google is directly liable for hallucinations in its AI Overviews, rejecting the platform immunity defense.

## Implications
This shift will force AI labs to move away from "probabilistic" summaries and implement much more aggressive, verifiable grounding and citing mechanisms. It creates a massive legal risk for any AI product that synthesizes information without a direct, traceable source for every claim.
