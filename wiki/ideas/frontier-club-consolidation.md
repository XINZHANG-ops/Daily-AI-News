# Frontier Club Consolidation

## The Insight
The "Frontier Club" refers to the tight-knit oligopoly of OpenAI, Google DeepMind, and Anthropic. These entities are transitioning from commercial competitors into an unofficial global regulatory body, coordinating their influence to ensure the rules of AI governance are written by those who control the most compute.

## Evidence
- [[topics/ai_governance]] — The coordinated presence of the top three CEOs at the G7 summit in France to discuss "global AI governance."
- [[ideas/regulatory-capture-via-safety]] — The joint advocacy for synthetic DNA regulations to redirect scrutiny away from their model weights.
- [[sources/openai]] — Sam Altman's "strategic double game" with the EU: combining technical compliance with threats of market exit.

## Implications
1. **Compute as Political Capital**: Control of the compute cluster (e.g., Google's 25% global share) is now translated directly into diplomatic and regulatory power.
2. **Cooperative Rivalry**: While they compete for users, they cooperate on "safety" and "governance" to prevent the democratization of frontier capabilities.
3. **Institutionalization of the Gap**: By setting the "safety bar" extremely high, the Frontier Club ensures that only they have the resources to meet the standards, effectively outlawing independent frontier development.
