# AI as Utility Layer

## The Insight
Three converging shifts on May 24, 2026 signal AI is becoming a utility layer where differentiation moves from model performance to infrastructure, governance, and pricing: (1) MCP protocol victory with enterprise governance (Microsoft .NET), security (Anthropic Tunnels), and infrastructure (Oracle) layered on top; (2) Google's Gemini 3 at $0.53/M input tokens undercuts everyone, forcing a race to the bottom on frontier model pricing; (3) Platform dependency unraveling as Microsoft-OpenAI exclusivity ends, Apple goes multi-model, and Meta positions Llama 4 as the open alternative.

- [[sources/apple]] — iOS 27 multi-model strategy: Siri now calls GPT, Gemini, and Claude—not just OpenAI; platform dependency unraveling
- [[sources/openai]] — Microsoft-OpenAI exclusivity ends; OpenAI can now serve across any cloud provider including AWS and Google Cloud
- [[entities/llama-4-405b]] — Meta's open-weight release fills the gap as Google goes proprietary and OpenAI delays GPT-5; offers alternative to closed APIs

## Implications
The net effect is clear: AI is becoming a utility layer. Model performance is still important, but it's no longer the primary differentiator. Instead, differentiation moves to:
- **Infrastructure**: Who provides the most reliable, scalable deployment?
- **Governance**: Who provides enterprise-grade controls (Microsoft .NET)?
- **Security**: Who provides safe execution (Anthropic Tunnels)?
- **Pricing**: Who offers the best cost-performance ratio?

The next battleground isn't the model—it's the stack around it. Labs that focus only on model performance will lose to those that build complete stacks.
