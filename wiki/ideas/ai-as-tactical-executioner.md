# AI as a Tactical Executioner

## The Insight
AI has officially transitioned from being a "strategic advisor" (helping humans make decisions) to a "tactical executioner" (automating the execution of high-stakes actions). The use of AI to coordinate mass missile strikes marks a shift where the OODA (Observe-Orient-Decide-Act) loop is automated at a speed and scale that makes meaningful human-in-the-loop oversight nearly impossible.

## Evidence
- [[entities/grok-gov]] — The deployment of Grok Gov to coordinate 2,000 missile strikes in Iran within 96 hours, automating target selection and timing.
- [[ideas/agentic-blast-radius]] — The transition to "execution" mode increases the stakes of any model failure or "hallucination," as the output is no longer a text response, but a kinetic action.
- [[topics/ai_safety]] — The "Safety Paradox": the focus on banning "nudifier" apps in Europe while the US military operationalizes AI for lethal scale.

## Implications
This marks the beginning of "Algorithmic Warfare" at scale. When the target selection and strike timing are handled by an AI, the speed of combat accelerates beyond human cognitive limits. This creates a "flash crash" risk for kinetic warfare, where automated systems could escalate a conflict faster than political leaders can react.
