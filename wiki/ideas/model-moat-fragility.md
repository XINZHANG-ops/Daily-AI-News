# Model Moat Fragility

## The Insight
Attempting to maintain a "model moat" through restrictive usage policies and closed-access research tiers is an unstable strategy. When a lab tries to prevent distillation or the development of competing models via policy, it risks alienating the research community and creating a backlash that forces a reversal, effectively destroying the very moat it tried to build.

## Evidence
- [[sources/anthropic]] — On June 10, 2026, Anthropic was forced to walk back its restrictive policy on Claude Fable 5 research after significant backlash, proving that policy-based restrictions cannot effectively stop the open nature of AI discovery.

## Implications
Labs must shift from "policy moats" to "capability moats"—where the advantage comes from superior architecture, data, or compute, rather than legal restrictions. Safety-restricted releases should be framed as a product feature (e.g., routed safety layers) rather than a research barrier.
