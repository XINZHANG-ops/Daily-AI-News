# Software-Defined Engineering

## The Insight
The shift from using AI as a coding assistant (fixing bugs, writing functions) to using it as an end-to-end engineering pipeline. In this paradigm, AI agents design, simulate, and optimize complex physical systems (like rockets) in a tight loop, potentially bypassing human bottlenecks in the design process.

## Evidence
- [[sources/spacex]] — SpaceX's $60B acquisition of Cursor to integrate agentic coding directly into aerospace workflows.
- [[entities/prometheus-ai]] — The creation of an "Artificial General Engineer" to bridge the gap between digital reasoning and physical execution.

## Implications
The bottleneck for high-value engineering shifts from "how to build it" (which AI handles) to "what to build" (which humans define). This could lead to an explosion in the speed of physical infrastructure development and a massive increase in the complexity of the systems that can be successfully engineered.
