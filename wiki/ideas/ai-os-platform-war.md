# AI OS Platform War

## The Insight
The competition between AI labs has shifted from a "Model War" (who has the highest MMLU/HumanEval) to a "Platform War" (who owns the OS layer of the user's life). This is being fought on two fronts: Personal Memory (the data layer) and Distribution Channels (the interface layer). Raw intelligence is becoming a commodity; the real moat is now "stickiness" through deep personal integration.

## Evidence
- [[entities/dreaming-v3]] — OpenAI's focus on high-recall personal memory to create a "sticky" user experience.
- [[sources/meta]] — The EU's order to open WhatsApp to rival AI chatbots, attacking Meta's distribution moat.

## Implications
The winner of the AI era will not necessarily be the one with the smartest model, but the one who successfully embeds their agent into the behavioral and data infrastructure of the user. "Intelligence" is the engine, but "Memory" and "Distribution" are the vehicle.
