# Compute Shortage Forces Cooperation

## The Insight
The compute shortage in frontier AI has become so severe that even ideological enemies must cooperate. On May 6, 2026, Elon Musk — who months earlier accused Anthropic of "hating Western civilization" — signed a deal giving Anthropic access to 300+ megawatts of compute capacity at SpaceX's Colossus 1 data center in Memphis. Simultaneously, Musk dissolved his own xAI and rebranded it as SpaceXAI. The deal solves Anthropic's acute compute shortage that had been causing service reliability issues, while giving SpaceX diversified revenue and a toehold in AI.

This is not a friendship. It is a structural imperative: when GPU/TPU supply is the binding constraint on trillion-dollar valuations, even the fiercest rivals become interdependent.

## Implications
The cooperation imperative rewrites the competitive map of AI. When infrastructure is scarce, moats shift from model quality to compute relationships. Anthropic now has guaranteed access from Google (TPUs), SpaceX (Colossus 1), and Amazon (AWS) — a diversified supply chain that insulates it from any single provider's failure. For SpaceX, the deal provides revenue diversification while the rocket business faces cyclical downturns.

The xAI dissolution signals that Musk recognizes standalone AI labs cannot compete without hyperscaler-scale infrastructure. By folding xAI into SpaceX and renting capacity to Anthropic, Musk is pivoting from "build a model company" to "own the infrastructure that model companies need." This is the same pattern as NVIDIA's Vera Rubin platform: in a shortage, infrastructure owners win regardless of which models succeed.
