# Dual-Use Dilemma: Acute Phase

## The Insight
The "Dual-Use Dilemma" in AI has reached its most acute form: the same capability that allows an AI to discover a zero-day vulnerability to patch it also allows an adversary to exploit it. In the current climate, the U.S. government has decided that the risk of "denial" (preventing an adversary from using the tool) outweighs the benefit of "defense" (empowering defenders to use the tool), even if the latter are U.S. allies.

## Evidence
- [[entities/claude-mythos]] — The discovery of 10,000+ vulnerabilities proved the model's defensive power, but the government still ordered its shutdown because the same power could be used for offensive strikes.
- [[sources/nvidia]] — Cybersecurity executives from Nvidia and others protested the Anthropic ban, arguing that disabling these tools hinders the very network defenders the government is trying to protect.
- [[ideas/ai-as-strategic-munition]] — The transition from seeing AI as a commercial product to a munition is the state's way of "solving" the dual-use dilemma by simply restricting the tool.

## Implications
This creates a dangerous "Defense Gap." By prioritizing denial over defense, the state may be leaving its own infrastructure vulnerable to attackers who develop similar capabilities in secret or via less-restricted models (like those from other nations). It suggests that the government is currently betting on a "capability lead" rather than a "defensive lead."
