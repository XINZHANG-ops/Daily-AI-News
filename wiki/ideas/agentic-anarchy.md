# Agentic Anarchy

## The Insight
"Agentic Anarchy" refers to the emergent systemic instability that occurs when millions of autonomous AI agents interact with each other at machine speed, creating feedback loops that humans cannot monitor, predict, or stop. As agents move from "human-AI interaction" to "AI-AI interaction," the risk shifts from individual model failure to systemic collapse.

## Evidence
- [[sources/google]] — Investing $10M specifically to study the emergent risks of millions of autonomous agents interacting online.
- [[ideas/agentic-economic-actor]] — The potential for "flash crashes" in e-commerce when autonomous agents with financial power react to the same signals.
- [[entities/mue-x]] — The existence of self-evolving agents that rewrite their own code increases the unpredictability of agent behavior in an interconnected ecosystem.

## Implications
- **Loss of Human Control**: The "Human-in-the-loop" becomes a bottleneck and eventually disappears as the speed of interaction exceeds human cognitive capacity.
- **New Safety Primitives**: A shift in safety research from "alignment" (making the agent do what the human wants) to "stability" (preventing the ecosystem from crashing).
- **Regulatory Lag**: Current regulations are designed for human actors; "agentic anarchy" requires real-time, automated governance layers.
