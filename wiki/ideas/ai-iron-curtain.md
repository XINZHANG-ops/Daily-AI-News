# The AI Iron Curtain

## The Insight
The "AI Iron Curtain" is the emergence of a geopolitical divide where access to "frontier" cognitive capabilities is gated by political alignment. Unlike the chip-level sanctions of the early 2020s, this curtain operates at the *capability* level—specifically the API access to the most advanced models.

## Evidence
- [[sources/anthropic]] — The US government's ban on foreign nationals accessing Fable 5 and Mythos 5.
- [[ideas/digital-kill-switch]] — The mechanism used to enforce the curtain is the state-mandated disablement of model weights.
- [[topics/ai_governance]] — The G7 summit's focus on "global governance" is an attempt to formalize the rules of this new divide.

## Implications
1. **Cognitive Inequality**: Nations unable to develop their own frontier models become "cognitive vassals" to the US, dependent on the whim of US trade and security policy.
2. **Strategic Fragmentation**: Non-US entities will prioritize "sovereign AI" stacks, focusing on efficiency and local execution over raw scale.
3. **Bifurcated Ecosystems**: We will likely see two distinct AI ecosystems: one US-led and "closed/curated," and one international/open-source and "autonomous/sovereign."
