# AI Layoff Template

## The Insight
Coinbase's May 5, 2026 announcement of 14% workforce cuts is the first explicit articulation of a restructuring playbook that every tech company is quietly drafting. Armstrong's "one-person teams" and "AI agent management" roles reveal the real dynamic: AI is not eliminating engineering jobs; it is eliminating the organizational overhead around engineering. The 14% cut is not about cost savings ($50-60M restructuring cost nearly offsets the salary savings); it's about organizational velocity. Coinbase is flattening because AI removes the coordination tax that justified middle management — when an engineer with AI tools can spec, code, test, and deploy a feature solo, the three-layer approval chain becomes dead weight.

## Implications
The "AI wrangler" role — setting guardrails, verifying outputs, handling edge cases — is the canary. Some human jobs will not be replaced by AI but converted into AI-management positions. This is not mass unemployment; it is mass restructuring. The risk is that the "AI productivity dividend" may be an excuse for layoffs during a downturn rather than genuine efficiency gains. Every tech company is watching Coinbase to see if the productivity claims hold up under independent scrutiny.
