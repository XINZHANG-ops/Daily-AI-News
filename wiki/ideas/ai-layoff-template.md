# AI Layoff Template

## The Insight
Coinbase's May 5, 2026 announcement of 14% workforce cuts is the first explicit articulation of a restructuring playbook that every tech company is quietly drafting. Armstrong's "one-person teams" and "AI agent management" roles reveal the real dynamic: AI is not eliminating engineering jobs; it is eliminating the organizational overhead around engineering. The 14% cut is not about cost savings ($50-60M restructuring cost nearly offsets the salary savings); it's about organizational velocity. Coinbase is flattening because AI removes the coordination tax that justified middle management — when an engineer with AI tools can spec, code, test, and deploy a feature solo, the three-layer approval chain becomes dead weight.

## Evidence
- [[sources/coinbase]] — Coinbase's 14% cut (~700 jobs) and "one-person teams" policy explicitly reframes roles as "managing AI agents" rather than traditional engineering; the $50-60M restructuring cost nearly offsets salary savings, proving the layoffs are about organizational velocity, not cost reduction
- [[sources/meta]] — Zuckerberg: "tasks that once required 50-100 people can now be handled by as few as 10"; MCI employee tracking captures behavioral data to train the AI replacing the workers being tracked
- [[sources/microsoft]] — Microsoft's 8,750 buyouts are the institutionalization of the AI layoff template: Copilot default across Office with 20M paid enterprise seats means the company no longer needs the coordination layer that once justified those headcount levels
- [[topics/llm_models]] — ARC-AGI-3 exposed that models can't reason about novel situations, yet they excel at pattern-matching tasks like coding — exactly the tasks that middle management coordination was built around
- [[entities/claude-code]] — Anthropic's ARR surged from $9B to $30B driven by Claude Code; Coinbase's "ship in days what took weeks" validates the productivity narrative
- [[entities/codex]] — OpenAI's 3M weekly Codex users represent the same 10x compression in software development timelines
- [[topics/agentic_ai]] — Agent verticalization (IBM Bob, Microsoft Legal Agent, Ruflo) means AI is not just augmenting individual tasks but replacing entire organizational functions

## Implications
The "AI wrangler" role — setting guardrails, verifying outputs, handling edge cases — is the canary. Some human jobs will not be replaced by AI but converted into AI-management positions. This is not mass unemployment; it is mass restructuring. The risk is that the "AI productivity dividend" may be an excuse for layoffs during a downturn rather than genuine efficiency gains. Every tech company is watching Coinbase to see if the productivity claims hold up under independent scrutiny.
