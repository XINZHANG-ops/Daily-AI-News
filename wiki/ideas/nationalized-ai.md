# Nationalized AI

## The Insight
The era of "Wild West" frontier AI development is over, replaced by an era of "Nationalized AI." In this paradigm, AI weights and infrastructure are no longer viewed as mere software products but as critical strategic assets—equivalent to nuclear technology or high-end semiconductors—subject to direct state control and geopolitical export restrictions.

## Evidence
- [[entities/claude-fable-5]] and [[entities/mythos-5]] — The June 15, 2026 global deactivation of these models by the U.S. Commerce Department via an emergency export control directive is the definitive proof of "kill-switch" power.
- [[sources/us_government]] — The transition of AI labs from "tech companies" to "quasi-state actors," as seen in Anthropic's technical team negotiating model restoration directly with White House officials.
- Political convergence in the U.S. (Trump and Sanders) on the idea of government equity stakes in AI firms, treating AI as a national utility like electricity or water.
- EU regulatory shifts that ban specific "immoral" AI (nudifiers) while strategically delaying high-risk regulations to maintain regional competitiveness.

## Implications
1. **The End of Globalism**: The "cloud" is being replaced by "digital borders." AI capability is now partitioned by geopolitical alignment.
2. **Strategic Vulnerability**: Reliance on a single nation's AI infrastructure is now a critical vulnerability, as evidenced by the global shock following the Anthropic shutdown.
3. **State-Led Direction**: The "red lines" for AI development are no longer set by lab safety boards alone, but by national security requirements and government directives.
