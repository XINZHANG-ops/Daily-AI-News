# AI Mathematical Discovery

## The Insight
May 22, 2026 marks the day AI moved from mathematical computation to mathematical discovery. OpenAI's reasoning model solved the Erdős unit distance conjecture by finding a counterexample, and Google DeepMind's AlphaProof Nexus solved 9 of 353 open Erdős problems and proved 44 OEIS sequence conjectures — all at $100-200 per problem, orders of magnitude cheaper than traditional mathematicians. This is no longer theoretical: AI reasoning systems can produce original mathematical research.

## Implications
The implications are profound. First, the economics of mathematical research are being disrupted — $100-200 per problem compared to thousands of dollars in human time means AI can explore vast spaces of conjectures that would be prohibitively expensive for human mathematicians. Second, the nature of mathematical work shifts: human mathematicians may become supervisors and validators of AI-generated proofs rather than primary discoverers. Third, this validates the reinforcement learning approach to reasoning — AI can learn to reason about problems it has never seen. The simultaneous achievement by both OpenAI and DeepMind on the same day signals that this is a capability frontier that will accelerate rapidly.
