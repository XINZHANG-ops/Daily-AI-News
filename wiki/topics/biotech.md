# AI in Biotech

## Overview
The application of AI to biology has transitioned from a supportive role to a generative one. The focus has shifted from optimizing known proteins to the "de novo" design of entirely new biological structures, drastically accelerating the cycle from AI hypothesis to clinical trials.

## Evolution
In June 2026, the field reached a milestone with the University of Cambridge launching the first human trials for a universal coronavirus vaccine designed entirely by AI. This marks the practical application of generative biology, where the "design" phase is fully automated, potentially reducing a process that previously took years to a matter of months.

## Patterns & Insights
**The hypothesis-to-trial compression**: The core pattern in generative biology is the drastic reduction in the iteration loop. By simulating protein interactions with high fidelity and using AI to design candidates from scratch, the need for iterative lab-based screening is minimized.
