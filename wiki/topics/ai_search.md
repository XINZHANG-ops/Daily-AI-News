# AI Search

## Overview
AI Search refers to the integration of generative AI into search engines, producing direct answers (rather than link lists) and enabling conversational follow-up. The category is led by Google's AI Overviews and AI Mode, with competitors from Perplexity, OpenAI, Microsoft Copilot, and Anthropic (Claude with search).

## Evolution
The AI search category emerged in 2024-2025 as a direct response to ChatGPT's threat to Google's link-based search monopoly. By mid-2026 the category has matured: Google has shipped AI Overviews at scale and is now adding publisher controls, Perplexity has carved out a premium "answer engine" niche, and OpenAI is integrating search into ChatGPT itself. The fundamental tension — AI search siphons traffic from publishers while Google still controls the underlying index — has become the defining political issue of the category.

The June 3, 2026 Google opt-out toggle in Search Console is the first major concession: publishers can now exclude their content from AI Overviews without affecting regular search placement. Whether this de-escalates or accelerates the open-web fracture is the open question.

## Patterns & Insights
- **The open-web fracture**: AI search and the publisher web have fundamentally misaligned incentives; opt-out toggles are a stopgap, not a resolution
- **Index dependency**: All AI search providers ultimately depend on the open web's content; publishers retain leverage they haven't yet used
- **Answer vs link**: The category war is over whether "answer" or "link list" is the right unit of search value
