# ENISA

## What It Is
ENISA (European Union Agency for Cybersecurity) is the EU's cybersecurity agency responsible for achieving a high and common level of cybersecurity across the EU. Based in Athens, Greece, ENISA works with EU member states and private sector stakeholders to enhance cybersecurity capabilities and support policy development.

## Key Facts

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Founded | 2004 |
| Location | Athens, Greece |
| Role | EU cybersecurity standardization and incident response |
| Key Function | Cybersecurity certification, threat analysis, policy advice |

## Significance
On June 2, 2026, ENISA became the first non-US public institution to receive access to Claude Mythos, Anthropic's cybersecurity-focused AI model. This access was granted after Claude Mythos autonomously discovered over 10,000 zero-day vulnerabilities across major operating systems and web browsers. The access marks a significant moment in AI cybersecurity geopolitics — allied nations with strong AI labs (like the US) get privileged access, while others must negotiate.

The discoveries made by Claude Mythos during ENISA access include:
- 27-year-old OpenBSD flaw
- 16-year-old FFmpeg vulnerability (automated tools tested 5 million times without finding)
- 17-year-old FreeBSD remote code execution

This highlights the emerging AI cybersecurity divide: organizations without access to frontier AI security tools are structurally disadvantaged against AI-powered attackers.
