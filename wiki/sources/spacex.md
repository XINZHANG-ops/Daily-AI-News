# SpaceX

## Overview
SpaceX acquired xAI for $250 billion in an all-stock transaction in April 2026, creating a $1.25 trillion "galactic AI entity." On May 6, 2026, SpaceX signed a compute partnership with Anthropic providing 300+ megawatts of capacity at SpaceX's Colossus 1 data center in Memphis — a surprising alliance given Musk's prior criticism of Anthropic. Simultaneously, xAI was dissolved and rebranded as SpaceXAI, consolidating Musk's AI bets under one roof. The combined entity aims to leverage Starlink's satellite network for low-latency global AI distribution and Tesla's robotics expertise for physical AI deployment. SpaceX (which owns AI assistant Grok) is now integrating advanced agentic coding into its core aerospace engineering workflows following the $60 billion acquisition of Cursor in June 2026.

On June 5, 2026 (disclosed in amended S-1), SpaceX signed a deal with Google for $920M/month from October 2026 through June 2029 (~$30-32B total) for access to ~110,000 NVIDIA GPUs at the former xAI Memphis data centers. The deal, sitting next to Anthropic's $1.25B/month deal, makes clear that the 2026 frontier is gated by who has signed the largest capacity contract — not who has the best model.
