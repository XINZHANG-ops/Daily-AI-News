# Recursive Superintelligence

## Overview
Recursive Superintelligence is an AI software company focused on self-improving AI systems. The company raised $650 million in May 2026 at a $4.65 billion valuation to develop "Level 1 autonomous training systems" — AI that can improve itself without human intervention. The company counts AMD Ventures and NVIDIA as investors in the same round, a notable given typical GPU supplier competition.

## Significance
The involvement of both AMD Ventures and NVIDIA in the same funding round is notable — typically these companies compete for AI compute mindshare. This suggests Recursive is positioning itself as hardware-agnostic, which is smart given the ongoing GPU shortage. The "Level 1 autonomous training" claim is bold — it implies the system can improve itself without human intervention.

However, compared to Isomorphic Labs' $2.1B raise the same week for drug discovery, "autonomous training" is harder to verify and has fewer near-term applications. The funding reveals investor appetite for diverse AI applications: drug discovery (concrete, measurable) vs. autonomous training (speculative, long-term).
