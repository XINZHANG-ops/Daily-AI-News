# Cambridge Neuromorphic Chip

## What It Is
A neuromorphic computing chip developed by scientists at the University of Cambridge, using hafnium-based memristors that mimic brain neuron connections. The device operates at switching currents a million times lower than conventional oxide-based memristors, potentially cutting AI energy consumption by up to 70%.

## Significance
This represents a significant breakthrough for sustainable AI development. As AI infrastructure consumes increasingly massive amounts of energy (Big Tech spending $650B in 2026), Cambridge's neuromorphic chip offers a path to dramatically reduce the carbon footprint and operational costs of AI computation. The brain-inspired architecture operates at near-threshold switching energies, enabling AI inference with substantially lower power draw.
