# Perceptron Mk1

## What It Is
Mk1 ("Mark One") is Perceptron AI's physical AI model for video understanding and embodied reasoning. Founded by former FAIR scientists, it matches frontier-lab performance at a fraction of the cost. The model targets manufacturing, robotics, geospatial, and security use cases where physical-world reasoning is required.

## Significance
Perceptron Mk1 launched the same day as Thinking Machines Lab's TML-Interaction-Small — both targeting real-time AI but from opposite directions. While TML optimizes conversational latency, Perceptron targets physical embodiment. This simultaneity reveals an industry-wide race to ship "agentic" systems before the category definition solidifies.

The model adds a third competitor to the AI cybersecurity race alongside OpenAI's Daybreak and Anthropic's Mythos. Perceptron's embodied agents approach cybersecurity from a physical-world angle — securing manufacturing floors, robotic systems, and geospatial infrastructure — rather than the software-centric approaches of OpenAI and Anthropic.
