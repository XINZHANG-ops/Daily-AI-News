# Microsoft Legal Agent

## What It Is
Microsoft Legal Agent is a specialized Copilot built for clause-by-clause contract review inside Microsoft Word, launched May 1, 2026. Unlike generic AI assistants with legal prompts, it's purpose-built for the legal workflow — understanding contract structure, flagging risky clauses, suggesting standard alternatives, and operating within the Word document where lawyers already work.

## Key Facts

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Release Date | May 1, 2026 |
| Creator | Microsoft |
| Platform | Microsoft Word (M365 Copilot) |
| Multi-Model | Supports Claude alongside OpenAI models |

## Significance
Legal Agent is the first concrete example of Microsoft's verticalization strategy: carving the enterprise into profession-specific AI surfaces. Instead of selling "Copilot can help lawyers," Microsoft ships a product that understands contract review specifically. This makes Copilot harder to displace than a generic AI assistant would be — a legal team using Legal Agent is embedded in the Microsoft ecosystem at a workflow level, not just a tool level. The multi-model support (Claude alongside OpenAI) is a strategic hedge: Microsoft gets the best model for each legal task without being dependent on any single AI provider. Legal Agent signals that the enterprise AI market is moving from horizontal platforms (one AI for everyone) to verticalized workflows (one AI per profession).
