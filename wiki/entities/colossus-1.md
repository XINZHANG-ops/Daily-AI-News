# Colossus 1

## What It Is
SpaceX's Colossus 1 is a 300+ megawatt data center in Memphis, Tennessee, housing 220,000+ NVIDIA GPUs. It is the physical infrastructure backing Anthropic's compute partnership with SpaceX, enabling the immediate doubling of Claude Code rate limits across all paid tiers and the removal of peak-hour throttling. In May 2026, Anthropic signed a landmark deal to pay SpaceX $1.25 billion per month for access to Colossus clusters, with total potential value reaching $45 billion through May 2029.

## Significance
Colossus 1 represents the physical reality of the compute shortage that is restructuring AI competitive dynamics. When Anthropic needed immediate capacity to solve service reliability issues, SpaceX had unused power at Colossus 1 — making the partnership possible. The 220,000+ GPU count in a single facility is unprecedented scale, and it gives Anthropic dedicated capacity that OpenAI lacks.

The May 2026 deal at $1.25B/month ($15B/year) equals approximately 80% of SpaceX's 2025 revenue, making AI compute a material business line for the first time. At this scale, SpaceX's compute revenue could soon rival its launch business — a fundamental shift in the company's business model. The 90-day termination clause suggests both parties see this as a transitional arrangement: SpaceX may be building its own training capability while Anthropic bridges to self-owned infrastructure.

This deal reshapes the AI infrastructure landscape: compute is becoming a commodity-like infrastructure business with real revenue potential. Even well-funded AI labs are willing to pay premium for guaranteed access, validating that compute scarcity is real and structural.
