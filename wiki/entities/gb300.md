# GB300 Blackwell Ultra

## What It Is
NVIDIA's latest GPU architecture, entering mass production at TSMC's N3P node in late April 2026 with shipments to priority hyperscaler customers (Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud, Meta) beginning in May 2026. The GB300 ( Blackwell Ultra) delivers 288GB HBM3e memory, 10 TB/s bandwidth, and NVLink 6, providing 50x better throughput per megawatt for agentic AI workloads compared to the previous Hopper generation.

## Significance
The GB300 Blackwell Ultra's 35x lower cost-per-token for agentic AI workloads directly addresses the economics exposed by GitHub's 275M commits/week and the $49/agent/month Copilot pricing. If GB300 NVL72 can handle agent-scale coding at 1/35th the cost, GitHub Copilot's pricing starts looking expensive — and the economics of autonomous coding become viable for smaller players.

The 50x throughput-per-megawatt improvement is equally significant: the energy constraint that has been bounding AI compute scaling becomes less binding, at least for companies with priority allocations.

However, TSMC's 3nm yield crisis (dropping to 52% for H200 wafers) means GB300 supply will be constrained to first-wave customers (Microsoft, Google, Meta). AWS and smaller labs wait until Q3. This creates a compute stratification: companies with GB300 allocations vs. those without will face a widening cost gap in Q3-Q4 2026.
