# Prithvi

## What It Is
NASA's Prithvi is the first geospatial AI foundation model deployed in space, uploaded to the Kanyini satellite and the International Space Station (IMAGIN-e payload). Trained on 13 years of Landsat and Sentinel-2 data, it processes Earth observation imagery in orbit for flood detection, disaster monitoring, and crop prediction — transmitting insights instead of raw data to save bandwidth.

## Significance
Prithvi represents a category shift: AI models are becoming orbital infrastructure, not just software. By processing satellite imagery in space before transmitting to ground, NASA solves the bandwidth bottleneck that has constrained Earth observation for decades. The 13-year training dataset gives Prithvi temporal depth no commercial model can match — NASA is leveraging its unique data moat.

The dual deployment strategy is telling: Kanyini tests standalone orbital AI, while ISS allows human-in-the-loop experimentation. The broader implication: if models can run on radiation-hardened, power-constrained space-grade hardware, terrestrial edge deployment becomes trivial by comparison. This also previews a new geopolitical battleground — "AI in orbit" could become as strategically significant as GPS or satellite communications.
