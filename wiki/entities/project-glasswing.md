# Project Glasswing

## What It Is
Project Glasswing is a restricted-access cybersecurity consortium launched by Anthropic to share the defensive insights derived from Claude Mythos discoveries. As of June 2, 2026, the coalition expanded from 30 to 150 organizations across 15+ countries, including NATO and Samsung. The consortium receives controlled access to Claude Mythos's vulnerability discoveries, with Mythos-class models promised to all customers "in the coming weeks."

## Significance
Project Glasswing represents a new model for AI safety — instead of releasing dangerous capabilities broadly, Anthropic created a closed consortium for responsible sharing. This approach acknowledges that Claude Mythos's vulnerability discovery capabilities are too valuable to waste (thousands of real vulnerabilities found) but too dangerous for open release.

**June 2, 2026 — The 150-Org Expansion**: Glasswing going from 30 to 150 organizations in one quarter is the closest thing the AI industry has to an enterprise FOMO signal. The inclusion of Samsung signals on-device licensing — likely a Mythos-distilled 3-7B for Galaxy AI. NATO as a launch partner is the headline: military-grade deployment implies the Mythos class has cleared export-control and dual-use review at a level Claude Opus 4.8 hasn't, which means capability is now sufficiently far ahead of the public tier to warrant classification. The "coming weeks" language combined with the June 3 Trump EO on voluntary model review suggests Anthropic is racing to get Mythos public BEFORE the federal testing window becomes mandatory, so the launch can frame the narrative rather than respond to it.

The consortium approach has implications for how frontier AI capabilities are governed: government pressure (CISA discussions, emergency CEO calls) suggests that even restricted releases require coordination with national security interests.
