# Hatch

## What It Is
Hatch is an agentic shopping tool Meta is developing for Instagram, targeting a June 2026 internal launch. It reportedly started development using Claude models before planning to switch to Meta's own Muse Spark model at launch — an implicit admission that Meta's proprietary models aren't yet competitive for agentic tasks.

## Significance
Hatch reveals Meta's agentic strategy at a critical moment. The company is spending $125-145B in capex and cutting 8,000 jobs to become "AI-first," yet its own models can't power the agentic products it wants to ship. The planned switch from Claude to Muse Spark is a form of model substitution that Meta hopes will close the capability gap by June — but the need to start with Claude suggests Muse Spark is significantly behind on agentic reasoning.

This is the agentic catch-up game in miniature: Meta sees OpenAI and Anthropic shipping agentic products (Claude Code, Codex, Operator) and knows it must respond. But without competitive models, Meta is forced to either use competitors' models (which it doesn't want to do) or ship inferior products. Hatch's success or failure will be an early signal of whether Meta's massive capex spending can translate into competitive agentic capabilities in 2026.
