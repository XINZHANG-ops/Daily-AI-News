# GPT-5.5-Cyber

## What It Is
GPT-5.5-Cyber is OpenAI's cybersecurity variant built on the GPT-5.5 flagship model. Released with restricted access on May 1, 2026, to verified defenders only, it includes binary reverse engineering and local reasonability capabilities for defensive security work. On May 12, 2026, OpenAI expanded the Trusted Access for Cyber (TAC) program to EU institutions, granting them access to GPT-5.5-Cyber capabilities.

## Significance
On May 1, 2026 — after Sam Altman publicly mocked Anthropic's Mythos restrictions as "fear-based marketing" — OpenAI imposed identical restrictions on GPT-5.5 Cyber, restricting it to verified defenders only. The reversal reveals liability now overrides competitive posturing for both labs.

On May 12, 2026, OpenAI expanded the Trusted Access for Cyber program to EU institutions, granting them access to GPT-5.5-Cyber. Former UK Chancellor George Osborne positioned this as a geopolitical win: "Europe's many defenders, not just the few." This EU expansion contrasts sharply with Anthropic's refusal to grant EU regulators preview access to Claude Mythos despite 4-5 meetings and public demands from Germany's Bundesbank.

GPT-5.5-Cyber is the foundation for OpenAI's Daybreak cybersecurity platform, which launched the same day as the EU expansion. It partners with CrowdStrike, Palo Alto Networks, Cloudflare, Cisco, Oracle, Fortinet, Akamai, and Zscaler to integrate GPT-5.5's security capabilities into enterprise security stacks.
