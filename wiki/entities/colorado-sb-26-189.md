# Colorado SB-26-189 AI Act

## What It Is
Colorado's "AI Act" signed by Governor Jared Polis on May 14, 2026, replacing the state's 2024 AI law. It mandates notifying individuals when AI is used in consequential decisions (employment, healthcare, housing). Takes effect August 12, 2026.

## Significance
SB-26-189 replacing the 2024 law just two years later shows how quickly AI regulation is iterating. The bipartisan support signals that AI oversight is not partisan — it is about covering legislative gaps as harms become visible. Colorado's approach — disclosure over prohibition — gives businesses flexibility while protecting consumers.

The notification requirement mirrors GDPR's transparency principles but adds enforcement teeth with an August 2026 deadline. Compare to the federal stalemate: while Washington debates which agency should oversee AI, states are writing actual rules. This creates a patchwork where businesses operating nationally must comply with Colorado's requirements even if other states have no equivalent.

The timing — same week as YouTube's deepfake detection expansion — suggests coordinated industry response to the fragmented US regulatory landscape. Unlike watermarking proposals that target generative models, Colorado's law targets the use layer: the decision, not the model.
