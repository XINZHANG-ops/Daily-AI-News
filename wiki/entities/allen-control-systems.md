# Allen Control Systems

## What It Is
Allen Control Systems is a US defense technology company that raised a $200M Series B at a $2.2B valuation on June 8, 2026, led by Smash Capital with Craft Ventures, Rally Ventures, and Inspired Capital participating. The company produces Bullfrog, an AI-guided autonomous counter-drone system that fuses computer vision, robotics, and AI decision-making to track and neutralize small unmanned aerial systems. Bullfrog is already deployed with the US Army and US Navy and achieved a 100% success rate in the T-REX 26-1 defense exercise.

## Significance
The Series B is the clearest single funding event of June 2026 in defense AI outside the Pentagon's classified contracts. The $2.2B valuation for a counter-drone specialist represents the AI-defense thesis crystallizing at the venture-scale layer: computer vision + robotics + autonomous decision-making is now a category with its own venture-scale capital. The 100% T-REX 26-1 success rate is the kind of empirical result that procurement officers cite when justifying rapid acquisition — the Bullfrog deployment is no longer a pilot.

The lead investor (Smash Capital) and the $2.2B post-money signal that the defense-AI category has graduated from "frontier tech" to "procurement-validated" in the venture mindset. Compare to the 2026 funding landscape: the same week that agent-harness founders raise seed rounds at $5M ARR, Allen Control Systems raises $200M at $2.2B. The capital allocation reveals where venture returns are expected to compound fastest — physical AI for defense, not virtual AI for SaaS.

The Bullfrog deployment also demonstrates that the AI-cybersecurity market Anthropic and OpenAI have been fighting over (the Mythos/Daybreak/TAC axis) has a parallel physical-AI counter-drone axis. The same underlying capabilities — computer vision, real-time decision-making, autonomous action — that power Bullfrog are the same capabilities Claude Mythos uses for vulnerability discovery. The defense AI market is bifurcating into "cyber" (Mythos class) and "physical" (Bullfrog class) with separate venture-scale funding paths.
