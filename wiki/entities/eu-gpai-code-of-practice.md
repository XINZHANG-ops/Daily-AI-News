# EU GPAI Code of Practice

## What It Is
The EU GPAI (General Purpose AI) Code of Practice is the operational framework that implements the EU AI Act's obligations for general-purpose AI providers. Originally published in late 2025 as a voluntary compliance framework, it shifted to a monitored regime on June 9, 2026, when the EU AI Office designated National Competent Authorities (NCAs) in EU member states to enforce compliance. Penalties for non-compliance reach €35M or 7% of global turnover, making the GPAI Code of Practice the first frontier-AI safety regime with both active enforcement and a credible penalty structure.

## Key Facts

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Parent Legislation | EU AI Act |
| Operational Body | EU AI Office |
| Enforcement Body | National Competent Authorities (NCAs) of EU member states |
| Status (Late 2025) | Voluntary framework |
| Status (June 9, 2026) | Monitored; NCAs designated |
| Penalty Ceiling | €35M or 7% of global turnover |
| First Frontier Model to Ship Under Monitoring | Claude Fable 5 (Anthropic, June 9, 2026) |

## Significance
The shift from voluntary to monitored is the first concrete enforcement of frontier-AI safety obligations by any major jurisdiction. Until June 9, 2026, every frontier-AI safety regime was either:
- **Voluntary** (US NIST frameworks, GPAI Code of Practice pre-June 9)
- **Aspirational** (China's state-directed frameworks without specific enforcement bodies)
- **Domain-specific** (Pentagon's classified AI coalition, EU's cyber-specific TAC program)

The NCA designation makes the EU the first jurisdiction with a multi-domain, multi-provider, active-monitoring framework backed by a credible penalty structure. The 7% of global turnover ceiling is calibrated to be material to trillion-dollar AI labs: a 7% fine on a $1T annual revenue lab is $70B, which is in the range where compliance becomes existential rather than a line item.

The Fable 5 launch on the same day as the NCA designation is structurally significant: the first frontier model to ship under active monitoring is also the first model to be productized with a 5% safety-routing layer. The EU will be the first regulator to test whether "5% safety routing" satisfies the GPAI Code of Practice's obligations, or whether stricter capability restrictions are required. The answer will set a precedent that every other jurisdiction will reference.
