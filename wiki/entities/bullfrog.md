# Bullfrog

## What It Is
Bullfrog is an AI-guided autonomous counter-drone system developed by Allen Control Systems. It fuses computer vision, robotics, and AI decision-making to track and neutralize small unmanned aerial systems. Bullfrog is deployed with the US Army and US Navy and achieved a 100% success rate in the T-REX 26-1 defense exercise. The system is the flagship product that drove Allen Control Systems' $200M Series B at a $2.2B valuation in June 2026.

## Significance
Bullfrog is the first AI-guided counter-drone system to demonstrate deployment-grade performance (100% success in T-REX 26-1) at the venture-scale funding tier. The system's $2.2B valuation is the empirical result that converts a pilot program into a procurement priority: the T-REX 26-1 success rate is the kind of metric that defense procurement officers cite when justifying rapid acquisition under existing acquisition authorities.

The computer vision + autonomous decision-making architecture parallels the capability stack of Claude Mythos (cybersecurity vulnerability discovery) but applied to physical airspace. The same underlying AI capabilities — real-time perception, action planning, autonomous execution — power both. The defense AI market is bifurcating into cyber (Mythos, Daybreak, TAC) and physical (Bullfrog, autonomous weapons) with separate venture-scale funding paths and separate regulatory frameworks.

The US Army and US Navy deployments are the procurement-grade signal: a system that the US military is actively operating is on a different acquisition trajectory than a research prototype. Bullfrog is now a candidate for the Pentagon's classified AI coalition (the eight-company group) but as of June 10 is not yet a member — winning the venture capital on empirical results rather than classified-network access.
