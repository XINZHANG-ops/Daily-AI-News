# Cosmos 3

## What It Is
NVIDIA's physical AI foundation model launched May 31, 2026 — the first fully open omnimodel with native vision reasoning and multimodal generation for physical AI. Available in two variants: Super (32B parameters) for high-performance workloads and Nano (8B parameters) optimized for edge deployment on robots.

## Significance
Cosmos 3 isn't just another LLM — it's explicitly designed for robotics and physical world interaction. The 8B Nano variant specifically optimized for edge deployment on robots represents NVIDIA's bet that physical AI (robots, autonomous vehicles, industrial systems) is the next frontier.

Combined with the new Agent Toolkit (NemoClaw blueprints, OpenShell runtime), NVIDIA is positioning itself as the infrastructure layer for the coming wave of physical AI agents. The open model strategy contrasts with Google's closed approach and directly challenges OpenAI's software-centric worldview.

The timing with OpenAI's robotics hiring announcement and NVIDIA's Cosmos 3 launch isn't coincidental — both companies see physical AI as the next big opportunity.
