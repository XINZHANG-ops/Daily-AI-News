# CAISI

## What It Is
CAISI (Center for AI Standards and Innovation) is a NIST division within the US Commerce Department that conducts pre-deployment safety and security evaluations of unreleased AI models. Google, Microsoft, and xAI agreed on May 5, 2026 to give CAISI early access to their frontier models for evaluation in classified environments. OpenAI and Anthropic are also participating, having renegotiated Biden-era agreements to align with Trump's AI Action Plan.

## Significance
CAISI represents the most consequential AI policy development of 2026: the shift from voluntary safety frameworks to structured government pre-release evaluation. The "voluntary with teeth" design — companies joined because the alternative was likely a mandatory executive order — proves this is not theater. The parallel with FDA drug trials is intentional: CAISI is positioning itself as the approval gate for AI "therapeutics" that could harm national security. But the governance gap remains enormous — CAISI has ~200 staff and 40 evaluations completed, while frontier labs will ship 500+ model versions this year. The question is not whether pre-testing works, but whether it can scale faster than model development.
