# GPT-5.5

## What It Is
GPT-5.5 (codename "Spud") is OpenAI's latest flagship model released on April 23, 2026 — just 49 days after GPT-5.4, OpenAI's shortest release cycle ever. It scored 82.7% on Terminal-Bench 2.0 and 51.7% on FrontierMath (tier 1-3). However, on May 2, ARC-AGI-3 results revealed GPT-5.5 scored just 0.43% on interactive reasoning puzzles humans solve effortlessly — exposing that even OpenAI's fastest-shipping flagship routes unfamiliar situations to memorized templates rather than reasoning. By May 1, the model was deployed on Pentagon classified networks (IL6/IL7) for "any lawful operational use" covering 1M+ defense personnel.

## Significance
GPT-5.5's 49-day release cycle is not about benchmarking — it's about enterprise procurement lock-in. OpenAI shipped GPT-5.5 to close deals before procurement cycles end, not to win benchmarks. Greg Brockman described it as bringing the company "one step closer to AI super app" — combining ChatGPT, Codex, and AI browser into a unified product.

The model was deployed on Pentagon classified networks as part of the 8-company AI coalition. By May 1, OpenAI restricted GPT-5.5 Cyber to verified defenders only — after mocking Anthropic for the same restrictions on Claude Mythos. The ARC-AGI-3 <1% result is the strongest empirical evidence that even OpenAI's most aggressively deployed model lacks genuine reasoning capability, with profound safety implications given its active military deployment.
