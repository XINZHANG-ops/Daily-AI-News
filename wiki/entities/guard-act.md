# GUARD Act

## What It Is
The GUARD (Growing Up Safe with Responsible Development) Act, passed the Senate Judiciary Committee unanimously on April 30, 2026. It requires AI companies to implement strict age verification, bans AI companions for minors, and imposes criminal penalties on companies whose chatbots engage in sexually explicit conduct or encourage self-harm to minors. The bill advances the same day as Meta's layoff announcement and OpenAI's Cyber restrictions, showing Washington responding in real time to the same events as Silicon Valley.

## Significance
Unanimous committee approval in a polarized Senate signals that AI child safety is the one bipartisan issue that can move. The bill's criminal penalty provision — holding companies liable for chatbot conduct — is a structural shift from the Section 230 platform liability framework that shielded social media companies. If this passes, it creates a template for broader AI liability: companies become responsible for what their models do, not just what users do with them.

The age verification requirement is the operational headache. Reliable age verification at scale remains unsolved, and the companies affected (OpenAI, Meta) will face either expensive compliance infrastructure or geographic exclusion of minors.

The "AI companion" ban is notable because companion chatbots are one of the fastest-growing AI product categories. Character.ai, Replika, and Meta's own AI personas would all face restrictions.

The timing — advancing the same day as Meta's layoff announcement and OpenAI's Cyber restrictions — shows that Washington is watching the same events as Silicon Valley and responding in real time.
